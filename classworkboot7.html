Alappuzha

(Alleppey), Kerala, is known as the "Venice of the East" for its stunning network of backwaters, canals, and rivers, offering serene houseboat stays and traditional boat races like the Vallomkali. Located between the Arabian Sea and Vembanad Lake, this district boasts beautiful beaches, coconut-based industries (coir, oil), and a rich history as a former trade center, blending natural beauty with cultural heritage.

Kovalam

is a world-renowned beach destination in Kerala, famous for its three crescent-shaped beaches (Lighthouse, Hawa, and Samudra) separated by rocky promontories, creating calm, ideal waters for swimming. Popular since the 1930s, it offers sunbathing, massages, yoga, and various water sports, alongside numerous budget to luxury hotels, Ayurvedic resorts, shops, and restaurants serving South Indian and Continental food, making it a diverse tourist hub known for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Thiruvananthapuram

(Thiruvananthapuram) offers a mix of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, highlighted by the grand Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, serene beaches like Kovalam and Shankhumugham, and cultural sites such as the Napier Museum and Kuthiramalika Palace. Nature lovers can explore Neyyar Dam, Veli Tourist Village, and the nearby hill station Ponmudi, while the city also boasts diverse wildlife at the Thiruvananthapuram Zoo and rich art at the Sri Chitra Art Gallery, making it a vibrant destination for culture, relaxation, and nature.
